The Gundled Gazette was an 8-page newspaper developed for the New Year's edition of Zero Chill, a warehouse rave in Brownsville (New York City) organized by Seva Granik and his cronies. The concept for the party was a sort of opaque and gruesomely yassified real-estate-development inspired gentrification nightmare (in other words, 2025).

We sourced classified ads from ravers in and around the city and invited two beloved writers to collaborate on articles ranging from an internecine tabloid-style hit piece (Kay Gabriel) to a beautifully incomprehensible bastardization of real estate development marketing language (Ratcliff). Other ramblings included a contribution from renowned rat ethnographer and mal vivant McCalbie Fark (Eddie Baker) and our own analytical projections about the market.

The newspaper was stocked in stands throughout the warehouse space and provided to guests as they declarted in the afternoon. By the end of the party hundreds of these were strewn on the floor, laden with dirt and all sorts of unmentionable condiments.
